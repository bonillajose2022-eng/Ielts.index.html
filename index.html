What Are Suffixes?

A suffix is a group of letters added to the end of a base word (or root) to create a new word with a different meaning or grammatical function. Understanding suffixes is essential for building vocabulary, reading academic texts, and achieving high scores in exams like IELTS and TOEFL.

Suffixes can change a word's part of speech ‚Äî turning a verb into a noun, an adjective into an adverb, or a noun into an adjective ‚Äî and they often shift the word's meaning at the same time.
